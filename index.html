About Me

I am a medical student whose research focuses on orthopedic outcomes, postoperative risk prediction using machine learning, and disparities in orthopedic care delivery. My work has been presented at national and international conferences and published in peer-reviewed journals. I also co-founded Mobilize Africa, a nonprofit that delivers medical and educational supplies to clinics and schools in underserved regions in West Africa through partnerships with U.S. healthcare institutions.

Current Focus

Machine Learning & Deep Learning in Surgical Care

Applying advanced computational methods to clinical datasets to identify early predictors of postoperative complications, with the goal of improving risk stratification and decision-making in surgical care.

E. A. Rankin Scholar

Awarded E. Anthony Rankin Scholar by the Society of Military Orthopedic Surgeons for immersive rotation at Walter Reed National Military Medical Center.

Media Engagement

Featured on ABC4 WoodTV discussing Reach Out to Youth, an initiative that brings children from underserved communities into medical environments. I also engage in public-facing health education through media and digital platforms.
