About Me

Angel G. A. Prempeh is a medical student at Michigan State University College of Human Medicine with a strong interest in orthopedic surgery and outcomes-focused research. Born in Ghana, he moved to the United States during his teenage years. This experience shaped his commitment to equity, service, and data-informed clinical care.

His research spans sports medicine, health services research, and clinical epidemiology, with projects examining Achilles tendon injuries in National Football League athletes, machine learning approaches to postoperative risk prediction, and disparities in orthopedic care access across Michigan. He has also contributed to modeling studies, including work using differential equations to examine influenza prevalence. His research has been presented at national and international conferences and has resulted in peer-reviewed publications.

Beyond academics, Angel is the co-founder of Mobilize Africa, a nonprofit organization dedicated to strategically connecting medical and educational resources from U.S. institutions to underserved communities across Africa. Through this work, he aims to advance sustainable, equity-driven healthcare initiatives that extend beyond clinical settings.
